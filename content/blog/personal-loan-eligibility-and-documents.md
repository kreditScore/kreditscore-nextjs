## What lenders look at for eligibility

Banks and NBFCs typically review:

- **Monthly income** (salary slips or ITR)
- **Credit score** and past repayment behaviour
- **Job or business stability** — how long you have been in the same role or profession
- **Existing EMIs** — your total fixed obligations each month

## Common documents for salaried applicants

- PAN card
- Aadhaar / address proof
- Bank statements (often 3–6 months)
- Salary slips
- Office ID or offer letter (depending on the lender)

## Self-employed / professionals

**ITR**, **business proof**, and **bank statements** usually carry more weight. Some lenders offer **dedicated programs** for profiles such as CAs, doctors, and teachers.

## Tips to improve approval odds

1. Work on your score or **dispute incorrect entries** on your report.
2. Understand your **total debt burden** — too many active loans can make your debt-to-income ratio look stretched.
3. Choose a **loan amount and tenure** so the EMI fits comfortably within your income.

## Final note

Every lender has different policies. **Pre-approval** or **eligibility checks** can give you a sense of what you may qualify for before you submit multiple full applications.
